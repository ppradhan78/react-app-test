Step 1: Identify business domains in Northwind

If you look at Northwind tables, they naturally group into bounded contexts.

Core domains in Northwind

1. Catalog / Product domain
Responsible for what you sell.

Tables:

Products

Categories

Suppliers (sometimes shared, sometimes owned here)

Microservice:
Product Service

Manage products

Manage categories

Product pricing

Product availability

2. Sales / Order domain
Responsible for selling and order lifecycle.

Tables:

Orders

OrderDetails

Microservice:
Order Service

Create orders

Calculate totals

Track order status

Apply discounts

3. Customer domain
Responsible for who you sell to.

Tables:

Customers

CustomerDemographics (optional)

Microservice:
Customer Service

Customer profiles

Customer addresses

Customer segmentation

4. Employee / HR domain
Responsible for internal users.

Tables:

Employees

EmployeeTerritories

Microservice:
Employee Service

Employee info

Reporting hierarchy

Sales assignments

5. Shipping / Logistics domain
Responsible for delivery.

Tables:

Shippers

Orders (shipping part only)

Microservice:
Shipping Service

Shipping providers

Shipment tracking

Delivery status

6. Geography / Reference domain
Usually a supporting domain.

Tables:

Regions

Territories

Microservice:
Region Service

Regions

Territories

Step 2: Assign database per microservice

Each microservice owns its own database schema (or its own database).

Example:

ProductDB
  - Products
  - Categories
  - Suppliers

OrderDB
  - Orders
  - OrderItems

CustomerDB
  - Customers

EmployeeDB
  - Employees

ShippingDB
  - Shippers
  - Shipments

RegionDB
  - Regions
  - Territories
